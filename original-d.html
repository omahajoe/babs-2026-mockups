What Works

Strong Brand Identity & Unapologetic Voice

The BABs brand voice is immediately distinct. Within seconds, a visitor knows this isn't a generic conference. The name itself is a qualifying filter — people who are put off by it were never the right buyer. This is a genuine competitive advantage that most event brands spend years trying to manufacture.

Clear Date, Location & CTA Above the Fold

The hero section gets the essentials right: September 16–18, Fort Worth, TX, and a Buy Your Ticket button. Visitors don't have to scroll to find the basics. This alone puts BABS ahead of 40% of event websites that bury critical logistics.

Two-Tier Pricing is Smart

GA at $597 and VIP at $1,097 with clearly differentiated value (mastermind lunches, speaker showdown access, post-event check-in) is solid event pricing architecture. The VIP tier creates both an upsell path and a scarcity lever.

Sponsor Visibility

Diamond sponsors are prominently placed. The "Become a Sponsor" CTA with a clear value prop ("put your brand in front of Badass business owners across the country") serves the dual purpose of driving sponsor revenue and signaling credibility to attendees.

The Countdown Timer Creates Urgency

A countdown to the event date is a proven conversion driver. It shifts the visitor's mindset from "I'll think about it" to "time is running out." This is event marketing 101, and it's good to see it deployed.

What Doesn't Work

The Hero Section Wastes 70% of the Most Valuable Real Estate on the Internet

The current hero is mostly empty black space with a headline ("Stop Playing in Business") and a button. There's no value proposition. No proof. No emotional hook beyond attitude. A first-time visitor who doesn't know BABS has zero reason to keep scrolling. Best-selling events use the hero to answer three questions in 5 seconds: What is this? Why should I care? What do I do next? Right now, BABS only answers the third.

Zero Social Proof Above the Fold

85% Excellent ratings. 83% word-of-mouth discovery. 7th annual event. None of these appear until deep in the page — and some don't appear at all. Events that consistently sell out lead with proof because the #1 objection for any event is "Is this worth my time and money?" The current site makes visitors take that on faith.

No Speaker Section, Schedule, or Content Preview

The single biggest driver of event ticket sales (after social proof) is knowing who's on stage and what you'll learn. The current homepage has zero speaker information, zero session previews, and zero agenda structure. A visitor considering $597–$1,097 has no way to evaluate the content quality without leaving the site.

The "Network Without the Bullshit" Section Has No Substance

This section makes a bold claim but provides no specifics. How is the networking structured? What formats are used? What makes it different from any other event's networking? Best-selling events describe their networking format in concrete terms: roundtable matching, curated 1:1 meetings, industry-specific breakouts. BABS says "skip the small talk" but doesn't explain what replaces it.

The Page Structure Kills Conversion Flow

The current page goes: Hero → Countdown → Brief copy → Networking blurb → Sponsors → Pricing → More sponsors → Footer. There's no storytelling arc. No problem-agitation-solution. No objection handling. No testimonials. No "who is this for" section. The page reads like a brochure, not a sales page. Sell-out events structure their pages as persuasion sequences, not information dumps.

No Testimonials or Attendee Voices Anywhere on the Page

This is the most critical miss. BABS has 26 verified survey responses with quotes like attendees praising the atmosphere and actionable content. None of these appear on the website. In a world where 83% of your attendees come from word of mouth, the website should BE word of mouth — filled with real voices from real operators who've been in the room.

VIP Value Prop is Buried and Under-Sold

The VIP tier includes a 45-day post-event accountability check-in, application to Speaker Showdown, and mastermind lunches with speakers. These are genuinely differentiated benefits. But they're listed in plain text on a pricing card with no framing, no story, and no urgency. The VIP section needs its own narrative: "This is for operators who don't just want access — they want accountability."

Copyright Shows 2025 — Signals Neglect

Small detail, big signal. A site that says "© 2025" in March 2026 tells visitors the site isn't actively maintained. For a $597+ purchase, this erodes trust.

What Needs Improvement

Needs a Belief System, Not Just an Attitude

The current site has edge, but edge without a framework is just noise. Option D's Champions Code gives BABS something no other event has: a proprietary belief system that qualifies the room. Six rules that tell prospects "if this sounds like you, you belong here." This transforms the brand from "aggressive conference" to "a community with a code." That's the difference between a one-time event and a movement.

Add a "Who This Is For / Who This Isn't For" Section

The best-selling events in every category use exclusion as a sales tool. Telling people who shouldn't come makes the people who should come feel seen. BABS is perfectly positioned for this: "Not corporate. Not woo-woo. Not bro-culture. Something else entirely."

Restructure as a Persuasion Sequence

The page should follow a proven conversion flow: Hook (hero with proof) → Qualify (who is this for) → Differentiate (what makes this different) → Prove (testimonials + stats) → Show (what you'll experience) → Price (with framing) → Close (urgency + final CTA). Every section should answer the objection the visitor is thinking at that scroll depth.

Mobile Experience Needs Attention

Over 60% of event ticket research happens on mobile. The current site's large empty spaces, oversized text blocks, and lack of thumb-friendly navigation hurt conversion on smaller screens. The mock below addresses this with responsive design and touch-optimized CTAs.

Needs a Video or Visual Proof Element

Video testimonials exist in the BABS Testimonials folder. A 60-second highlight reel or even a single attendee video testimonial in the hero section would dramatically increase conversion. Events with video on the landing page see 20–40% higher conversion rates vs. text-only pages.
